## 8. Definite Integrals

Given: $f(x)=\sin x$ on interval $[0,\pi]$.

Task: compute area under curve from $x=0$ to $x=\pi$.

---

### 1) Formula used
- Definite integral for area: $A=\int_{a}^{b} f(x)\,dx$.
- Antiderivative of $\sin x$ is $-\cos x$.

### 2) Compute integral
$$A=\int_{0}^{\pi} \sin x\,dx = [-\cos x]_{0}^{\pi}$$ 
$$F(b) - F(a)$$

Evaluate boundaries:
- For $x=\pi$: $-\cos\pi = -(-1)=+1$.
- For $x=0$: $-\cos0 = -(1)=-1$.

Then:
$$A = 1 - (-1) = 2$$

---

### 3) Final answer
- Area under the curve is $2$.
