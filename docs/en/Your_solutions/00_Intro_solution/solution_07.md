# Section 0 — Mathematical Foundations
## 7. Logic & Series

Problem:
- Bicycle initial distance to wall: 10 m.
- Bicycle speed: 1 m/s toward wall.
- Fly speed: 2 m/s starting from bicycle, flying toward wall, bouncing instantly and changing direction each hit.
- Question: total distance fly travels before bicycle hits the wall (crushes fly).

---

### 1) Key observation
- Time for bicycle to reach wall: $t_{b} = \frac{10\,\text{m}}{1\,\text{m/s}} = 10\,\text{s}$.
- Fly flies continuously for this time until bicycle arrives.
- Fly's path can be treated as straight-line motion at constant 2 m/s with no pause.

### 2) Fly distance
- Fly distance $d_f = v_{fly} \cdot t_b = 2\,\text{m/s} \cdot 10\,\text{s} = 20\,\text{m}$.

### 3) Series alternative (optional)
- This problem also has classic infinite back-and-forth summation with segment distances: 20/3 + 40/9 + ... = 20.
- But the direct timing method is simplest and exact.

---

### 4) Final answer
- Total distance fly travels: $20\,\text{meters}$.
