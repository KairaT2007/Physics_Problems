## 4. Rearranging Formulas

Given the pendulum period formula:

$$T = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}$$

---

### 1) Formula used
- $T = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}$.

### 2) Solve for $g$:
1. Divide both sides by $2\pi$:
   $$\frac{T}{2\pi} = \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}$$
2. Square both sides:
   $$\left(\frac{T}{2\pi}\right)^2 = \frac{L}{g}$$
3. Expand the square:
   $$\frac{T^2}{4\pi^2} = \frac{L}{g}$$
4. Solve for $g$:
   $$g = \frac{4\pi^2 L}{T^2}$$

---

### 3) Final answer
- $g = \frac{4\pi^2 L}{T^2}$
